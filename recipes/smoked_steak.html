Steak


What is a smoked steak?

This is steak. Steak is a wonderful, meaty food that usually comes from a cow. There are many ways to cook a steak, but for this recipe, we will be smoking it and reverse searing it.

Ingredients

  • 1 New York Strip Steak
  • Salt
  • Freshly Ground Black Pepper
  • Onion Powder
  • Garlic Powder

Tools Needed

  • Smoker
  • Grill
  • Cutting Board
  • Meat Temperature Probe
  • Tongs
  • Plate

Steps

  1. Take your steak out of the package, and pat it dry completely with a paper towel.
  2. Lay your steak out on a cutting board.
  3. Season your steak to your liking with the salt, pepper, onion powder, and garlic powder.
  4. Stick the temperature probe in the steak.
  5. Light up your smoker and set the temperature to 250F or 275F.
  6. Once your smoker is up to temperature, lay your steak in the smoker and smoke the steak until it hits an internal temperature of 130F.
  7. Once an internal temperature of 130F is reached for the steak, remove it from the smoker for 3-5 minutes and place it on a plate.
  8. In the waiting time, light up your grill and get it extremely hot.
  9. Once that time has passed, lay your steak on the grill using tongs.
  10. After 3 minutes, flip the steak using your tongs.
  11. Leave the steak on the uncharred side for 3 minutes.
  12. Once that time has passed, rest your steak for another 3-5 minutes on a plate off the grill.
  13. After those 3-5 minutes, cut into your steak and enjoy!
